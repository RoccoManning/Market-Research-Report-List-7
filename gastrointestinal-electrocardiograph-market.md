<p><ul><li>Electrogastroenterography (EGEG)</li><li>Cutaneous Electrogastrography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The gastrointestinal electrocardiograph market encompasses various techniques for assessing gastric motility. Electrogastroenterography (EGEG) measures electrical activity in the stomach using electrodes placed on the abdominal surface, providing insights into digestive disorders. Conversely, cutaneous electrogastrography focuses on non-invasive skin electrodes to monitor gastric rhythms, allowing for a broader application in clinical settings. Both methods offer valuable diagnostic information, aiding in the management of gastrointestinal conditions and enhancing patient care through improved understanding of gastric function.</p></p>
