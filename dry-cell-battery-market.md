<p><ul><li>Primary Cell</li><li>Secondary Cell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dry cell battery market is primarily divided into two types: primary cells and secondary cells. Primary cells are single-use batteries that cannot be recharged, commonly found in applications like remote controls and flashlights. They rely on irreversible chemical reactions to generate power. In contrast, secondary cells are rechargeable batteries that can be used multiple times, such as lithium-ion and nickel-cadmium batteries, widely utilized in electronics and electric vehicles. Both types cater to different consumer needs and applications.</p></p>
