<p><p>Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DEXA) bone densitometers are categorized into axial and peripheral types. Axial bone densitometers are typically used in clinical settings to measure bone density in the spine and hip, providing comprehensive assessments for osteoporosis diagnosis. Peripheral bone densitometers, on the other hand, are portable devices used for measuring bone density in the wrist, heel, or fingers, making them suitable for screenings and assessments in non-traditional settings. Both types play crucial roles in bone health evaluation and management.</p></p>
