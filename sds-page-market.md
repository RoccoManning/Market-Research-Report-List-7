<p><p>SDS-PAGE, or Sodium Dodecyl Sulfate polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis, is a widely used laboratory technique for separating proteins based on their molecular weight. This method is essential in various applications, including protein analysis, purity assessment, and molecular weight determination.</p><p>The SDS-PAGE market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand in research and development across biotechnology and pharmaceutical industries. <p><p>The SDS-PAGE market encompasses three primary types: gels, reagents, and instruments. Gels serve as the medium for protein separation, typically composed of polyacrylamide. Reagents include SDS (sodium dodecyl sulfate), buffers, and stain solutions essential for denaturing proteins and visualizing them post-separation. Instruments consist of electrophoresis systems, power supplies, and imaging equipment necessary for conducting experiments. Together, these components facilitate protein analysis in research and clinical laboratories, driving advancements in biotechnology and life sciences.</p></p>
