<p><p>The lung cancer diagnostics market comprises various types of testing methodologies. Immunoassays detect specific proteins or antibodies in blood. Flow cytometry analyzes cell characteristics for abnormalities. Rapid tests provide quick results, commonly at the point of care. Molecular assays identify genetic mutations. Tissue arrays allow simultaneous analysis of multiple samples. Circulating tumor cells are isolated from blood to assess cancer presence. Pharmacodiagnostics guide targeted therapies based on individual responses. Biomarkers indicate disease presence or progression, with other methods addressing niche diagnostics.</p></p>
