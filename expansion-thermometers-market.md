<p><p>Expansion thermometers operate on the principle of fluid expansion with temperature changes. Liquid expansion thermometers utilize liquids, typically mercury or colored alcohol, which expand in response to heat, allowing for accurate temperature readings. Conversely, gas expansion thermometers use the expansion of gases, such as nitrogen or air, where gas volume increases with temperature, providing measurements through pressure changes. Both types are essential for diverse applications in industries, laboratories, and meteorological observations, offering reliable and straightforward temperature monitoring solutions.</p></p>
