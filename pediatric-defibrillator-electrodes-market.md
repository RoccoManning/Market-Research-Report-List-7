<p><p>The pediatric defibrillator electrodes market is divided into two main types: internal and external electrodes. Internal pediatric defibrillator electrodes are designed for use during surgical procedures or when a patient's heart requires direct intervention, providing precise electrical shocks to restore normal rhythm. In contrast, external pediatric defibrillator electrodes are used externally on the chest to deliver shocks during emergencies. Both types are crucial for effectively managing cardiac arrest in children, ensuring swift and safe resuscitation efforts.</p></p>
